Segmentation & Churn Risk Review
Source: Online Retail II · 2,671 accounts
Overview

Of 2,671 active accounts, 24.1% are classified as churned (no purchase in 90+ days). Risk is concentrated in two segments — At Risk/Lost and Regular — which together hold 98.7% of all accounts.

The Champions and VIP/Whale segments, though small in number, account for a disproportionate share of revenue and show near-zero churn risk.

Reading the Ledger
At Risk/Lost (835 accounts, 59.6% churn risk) — largest single risk pool. Individually low value ($460 avg), but the volume makes this segment worth a low-cost win-back campaign (email re-engagement, small discount).
Regular (1,802 accounts, 38.5% churn risk) — the priority segment. Largest group overall, moderate value ($1,210 avg), and meaningful churn risk. A 5-point reduction in churn here protects more revenue than any other intervention.
Champions & VIP/Whale (34 accounts combined, <0.001% churn risk) — extremely loyal, extremely high value ($16,958 and $103,392 avg). No retention spend needed; consider light-touch loyalty perks to keep it that way.
